ABOUT ME

In my scientific career as a chemist, I have significantly contributed to environmental chemistry, generating knowledge and promoting cultural diversity. I have made four important advancements in this field, addressing crucial questions on environmental preservation and offering new perspectives on current challenges.
I just completed my master's degree in Environmental Contamination and Toxicology which is a collaborative program offered by the University of Bordeaux (France), the University of Porto (Portugal), and the University of the Basque Country (Spain). This interdisciplinary program deepened my understanding of Environmental and Analytical Chemistry, Data Analysis in Environmental Sciences, Advanced Ecotoxicology, Environment and Public Health, and Biogeochemistry of Environmental Contaminants.
Additionally, I have published a groundbreaking book on adapting the periodic table to the Kichwa language, spoken by indigenous communities in Ecuador. This work promotes inclusivity and recognition of linguistic diversity in science. I have actively engaged in knowledge transfer through national and international conferences and lectures, showcasing my ability to develop compelling proposals and dedication to scientific research.
Beyond my scientific achievements, I have contributed to society through scientific outreach, volunteer programs, and the formulation of municipal ordinances to raise awareness and disseminate knowledge on environmental issues.

SELECTED PROJECTS

Develop of new analytical methods based on nanoparticles for the determination of emerging pollutants


Environmental Sciences Institute (ICAM), University of Castilla-La Mancha, Toledo, Spain

Master thesis project based on the Development of a new chemical analytical method based on magnetic nano-sorbents for the determination, isolation, and preconcentration of antibiotics in water samples

General objective: To develop and validate analytical methods for extraction and preconcentration of pollutants in samples of environmental interest using magnetic nano sorbent based on Stir Bar Sorptive Extraction (SBSE).
